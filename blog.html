Reflection on Citizen Four

November 30th, 2025 (due October 15 :[ )


Citizen Four was a pretty interesting documentary. Snowdin clearly approached the expose of the classified matieral with great care, approaching trustworthy media, encrypting messages, and commuting to a location in which he could not be immediately persecuted. The documentary was especially well created, it gave an unadultered way to observe what he did from his own eyes. He acted in not his own interest, but in the interest of what he thought was ethically sound. He clearly did not want the attention, but rather did not want anyone else to get blamed for his actions. I think that is kind of heroic.

I will say, I think it is crazy he didn't tell his immediate girlfriend at all what was going on. I mean, there is a chance he did, and he simply left it out of his conversations with the media to ensure she wouldn't be prosecuted, but If I were about to make that decision, I would have told my partner.

My biggest takeaway is that, sadly, very little has happened that actually protects the privacy of citizens. If anything, it has worsened, with the advent of AI, better facial regulation, and deregulation.

Reflection on Ethics Discussion

September 3rd, 2025


Its a rainy day!! Also silksong eve. Perfect weather for reflection, especially on what is a discussion on ethics. My own ethics are rooted in sustainability and doing the most good- some would say this is utilitarian, and while I agree to some extent, I would argue that I am not a pure utilitarian. After looking over the book, I would argue I am more of a rule utilitarian than anything, but more so when those rules pertain to protecting human life and the environment. We are very much an interconnected species with the environment. What we do all circles back around to the very place we live in. I do my best in my position to attempt to keep my actions in check with these ideals.

One student mentioned believing that if given the chance, everyone would cheat on an assignment. I've pondered this sentiment since he stated this. It goes along with the idea, are all people born evil? I do not think so. But this isnt to say that people are inherently always good, as I think good behaviors are taught. But bad behaviors are also taught. We are a largely a product of the world around us. I do not cheat. I have the chance to easily cheat on most assignments, but I do not. Perhaps this statement was said from a personal expierence this person had, but I argue that a persons good or evils is never so black and white.

Best Computing Experiences????

August 20th, 2025


HELLO! I am Blaez, greeting anyone who may have stumbled and/or purposefully clicked on this here blog. This is a required post for my senior seminar course, but who knows I might continue using this blog sometime after!!

Best experience in class?? I would have to say that would be the same as my worst expierence in class!! I am talking about none other than Operating Systems. What a lovely, interestingly indepth class, taught by Dr. Norris. The expierence I refer to is the Shell lab, where we had to create our own shell on a linux machine. Its a bit more barebones than the Systems 2 lab that some may have done, a lot less instructions on how to complete it. Long story short, I probably worked on that lab a good 30 hours that week. It was grueling, I was so pressed for time I begged people to take my work shifts and had to ask my linear alegebra teacher for an extension on a project. What a fun time. It turned out being pretty swell though, as I was actually really proud of my shell. Everything was nicely documented, and I quite enjoyed how I eloquently handled multiple problems that presented themselves.

My best out-of-class computing expierence would either be tied with my first telemetry tests for sunergy, or my recent hackathon at app. Long story short, the Telemetry was a project I worked on with my friend Ryan Densmore all last semester. We worked with XBees to send data from our schools solar car (ROSE) to the pits, which is super important for data collection during solar vehicle races. We hadn't told the team manager about our success, so the look on his face when observing the display at testing day was pure bliss. Apphack was also a really fun expierence. My team create sun.fun, which was a collection of games. Ryan and I worked on a twitch chat generator, except the messages were all generated from a llama model based on microphone input. During the presentation, we had twitch chat running, and it constantly would be commenting on what myself, ryan, nathan, or hannah would say. It was a very fun time.
